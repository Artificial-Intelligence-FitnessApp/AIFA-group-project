Turkey Pie Recipe



Difficulty Intermediate



Servings

6-8

Prep Time

30 mins.

Cook Time

1 Hr.


Ingredients:

2 tablespoons of butter

1 cup chopped onion

1 small onion,minced

2 stalks celery,chopped

2 carrots,diced

1 turkey,obv

3 tablespoons dried parsley

3 tablespoons all-purpose flavour

3 tablespoons dried parsley

1/2 cup of milk



Calories*

350.0

Fat*

19g

Carbs*

50g

Protein

9g

Method

Pre heat your oven to 190°C/375°F/gas 5. Put your bacon in a large pan on a medium heat and add your thyme leaves. Add a lug of olive oil and let it all fry off a few minutes. Add all of your prepped leeks and fry them off for about 3 minutes. Add a pinch of salt and pepper then pop the lid on top, turn the heat down to medium and let them cook away gently for 30 minutes, stirring every 5 to 10 minutes to make sure they don't catch. There's going to be enough moisture in the leeks to keep them happy in the pan so they should be soft and melt in your mouth once they're done.

When your leeks are ready, add the turkey meat to them and stir. If you've got a bit of stuffing mixed in there you can put that in too. Add the flour, mix it in well then pour in your stock and stir again. Add the crème fraîche then turn the heat up and bring everything back up to the boil. Have a taste and add a bit more salt and pepper if it needs it then turn the heat off. Pour the mixture through a sieve over another empty pan and let the gravy from the mixture drip into the pan while you roll out your pastry.

Get a baking dish roughly 22 x 30cm. Roll your pastry out so it's double the size of your dish. Crumble the chestnuts over the pastry then tear some of the leaves over them. Fold the other half of pastry on top then roll it out evenly so you have a rectangle big enough to cover your baking tray.

Spoon that thick leek mixture from your sieve into the pie dish and spread it out evenly. Lay your pastry on top, tuck the ends under then gently score the pastry diagonally with your knife. Add a pinch of salt to your beaten egg then paint this egg wash over the top of your pastry. Pop your pie in the oven for about 35 to 40 minutes or until the pastry is puffed up and golden brown. When the pie is ready, re-heat the lovely gravy and serve with your pie, along with some peas tossed in butter, lemon, salt and pepper and everyone's happy!



Fruit Salad with Pineapple mint syrup



Difficulty:easy



Servings

4

Prep Time

5 mins.

Cook Time

5 mins


Ingredients:

100g caster suger

1/2 teacup of water

1 bunch pineapple mint, finely chopped, optional

1 kg mixed fruit



Calories*

220

Fat*

0.4g

Carbs*

51.2

Protein

1.5g


Method

Pineapple mint is great in all sorts of desserts. Try it chopped and sprinkled over ripe melon for a refreshing summer dessert.

Place the sugar and a teacup of water in a saucepan and heat up. Stir until the sugar dissolves. For a fuller flavour, add half a bunch of whole pineapple mint stems to the hot syrup and leave to infuse until cool.

When the syrup has cooled down, remove the stems and add the peeled and chopped fruit. Chill well and add half a bunch of picked pineapple mint leaves.



Mushroom soup with stilton, apple & walnut croutes



Difficulty:Easy



Servings

4

Prep Time

2 mins.

Cook Time

15 mins


Ingredients:

100g caster suger

1/2 teacup of water

1 bunch pineapple mint, finely chopped, optional

1 kg mixed fruit



Calories*

405

Fat*

16.8g

Carbs*

51.2

Protein

13.5g


Method

Peel, halve and finely slice the onions and put them into the large pan with 2 tablespoons of olive oil . Crumble in the stock cube, add a pinch of salt and pepper, strip in the thyme leaves and squash in 2 unpeeled cloves of garlic through a garlic crusher . De-stalk the chestnut mushrooms and place the tops on the griddle pan, turning when charred . Tear the chestnut stalks and portobellos into the onion pan, add the rice and cook for a couple of minutes . Pour in 1 litre of boiling water and boil with the lid on

Cut 4 slices of ciabatta at an angle and add to the griddle pan . When charred on both sides, rub with a halved garlic clove . Coarsely grate or slice the apple into matchsticks and toss with the roughly chopped parsley and a little lemon juice . Place the chestnut mushrooms on the toasts, crumble over the stilton and walnuts, then pop under the grill until the cheese is melted


Use the stick blender to purée the soup to a consistency you like, then season to taste, if needed, and swirl in the cream and truffle oil . Top the toasts with pinches of apple and parsley and serve on the side
