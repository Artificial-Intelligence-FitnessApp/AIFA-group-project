USER's Fitness Program

Date: DD-MM-YYYY

Day: Monday

Program Week: 3 of 4

Day One - Monday

•Barbel Shoulder Press

  1. 1. Seated on bench, take a shoulder-width grip on the bar with your palm facing forward.
  2. 2. Lower the weight slowly(in front) until it touches your upper chest.
  3. 3. Push vertically upward until your elbows lock out.

•Dumbbell Front Raise

  1. 1. Hold a pair of dumbbells at arms' length by your sides, thumbs pointing forward.
  2. 2. Lift dumbbells out in front, up towards shoulder level, keeping your elbow stiff.
  3. 3. Lower the weight back down to the start position.

•Dumbbell Lateral Raise

  1. 1. Hold dumbbells at arms' length.
  2. 2. Raise arms out to the side in an arc until dumbbells reach shoulders.
  3. 3. Lower back down to hips

Day Two - Tuesday

•Barbell Upright Row

  1. 1. Hold a barbell at arms' length using an overhand shoulder-width grip.
  2. 2. Pull the bar vertically upward, raising the elbows to shoulder height.
  3. 3. Lower the bar slowly down to the arms' extended position.

•Incline Barbell Press

  1. 1. While seated on an incline bench, take a shoulder-width overhand grip on the bar.
  2. 2. Lower the weight slowly until the bar thouches your upper chest.
  3. 3. Push the bar stright up until your elbows lock out.

•Barbell Bench Press

  1. 1. Lying on a flat bench, take a shoulder-width overhand grip on the bar.
  2. 2. Lower the weight slowly down to touch the middle chest.
  3. 3. Push the bar straight up until your elbows lock out.

Day Three - Wednesday

•Wide-Grip Pull-down

  1. 1. Take an overhand grip on the bar with hands 6 inches (15cm) wider than shoulder width.
  2. 2. Pull the bar down to the upper chest, squeezing your lats.
  3. 3. Return the bar to the start position overhead

•Close-Grip Pull-down

  1. 1. Take an underhand (reverse) grip on the bar, with hands spaced 6 to 12 inches (15 to 30cm) apart.
  2. 2. Pull the bar down to the upper chest, squeezing your lats.
  3. 3. Return the bar to the start position, arms extended overhead.

•Machine Row

  1. 1. Grip the handles with arms extended in front, supporting your torso against the chest pad.
  2. 2. Pull the handles toward your upper abdomen, keeping your spine straight.
  3. 3. Return the weight to the start position.
